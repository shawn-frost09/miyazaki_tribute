Hayao Miyazaki

A Masterful StoryTeller

Hayao Miyazaki is one of Japan's greatest animation directors. The entertaining plots, compelling characters, and breathtaking animation in his films have earned him international renown from critics as well as public recognition within Japan. The Walt Disney Company's commitment to introduce the films to the rest of the world will let more people appreciate the high-quality works he has given the movie-going public.

He established his own animation studio Studio Ghibli in 1985.

Some of his greatest works are :-

  • 1988 - Tonari no Totoro (My Neighbor Totoro)

  • 1989 - Majo no takkyūbin (Kiki’s Delivery Service)

  • 1992 - Kurenai no buta (Porco Rosso)

  • 1997 - Mononoke-hime (Princess Mononoke)

  • 2001 - Sen to Chihiro no kamikakushi (Spirited Away)

  • 2004 - Hauru no ugoku shiro (Howl’s Moving Castle)

  • 2008 - Gake no ue no Ponyo (Ponyo)

  • 2013 - Kaze tachinu (The Wind Rises)

Japan got it's first Oscar

Miyazaki’s Sen to Chihiro no kamikakushi (2001; Spirited Away) captured the top prize at the 2002 Berlin International Film Festival and won best Asian film at the Hong Kong Film Awards. In his native Japan it won best picture at the 2002 Japanese Academy Awards and replaced Titanic as the top-grossing film in Japanese history. In the film, Chihiro, an ordinary if slightly spoiled young girl, wanders away from her parents and enters a realm of gods and magic. There, dubbed with the name Sen, she is forced to make do with her wits in an effort to reclaim her name and return to the human world.

Sen to Chihiro no kamikakushi aka Spirited Away (Japan 2001) is the first anime to win an Oscar, for Best Animated Feature, when it took the prize during the Academy Award ceremony at the Kodak Theater, Los Angeles, California, USA on 23 March 2003. At 124 minutes, Hayao Miyazaki's (Japan) blockbuster is also the longest movie to win in this category.

Legend's Never Die!

Miyazaki declared that Kaze tachinu would be his last feature-length film, and he began work on Kemushi no Boro (Boro the Caterpillar), a short film for the Ghibli Museum in Mitaka. Miyazaki’s retirement appeared to be temporary, however; in 2016 he announced that Kemushi no Boro would be expanded to a feature-length release. The film marked Miyazaki’s first project to be done entirely in computer animation.

In 2015 he received an honorary Oscar from the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences.
